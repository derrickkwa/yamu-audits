What Hims / Ro / LifeMD structurally cannot do

Telehealth commoditises a chronic-disease medication into an app transaction. That business model has a ceiling, and JumpstartMD's 14 physical clinics sit above it.

Moat 1

In-person monitoring. Weekly vitals, InBody body composition scan, labs on file. A telehealth platform can ship a prescription; it cannot deliver the clinical infrastructure that tracks whether the medication is working.

Moat 2

HRT + GLP-1 integrated care. No major telehealth player bundles hormonal and metabolic care under one clinical relationship. Building it requires clinicians, exam rooms, and labs: the physical infrastructure they have explicitly rejected.

Moat 3

17 years of practice tenure + 1.5M pounds lost. Credence-goods law: in categories where outcomes can't be verified pre-purchase, longevity substitutes for proof. No telehealth brand younger than 2020 can claim this.

Moat 4

Concierge PPO insurance service. Handles the paperwork for out-of-network reimbursement. Direct answer to VoC's #1 operational pain point in the category ("$99 cancellation fees at Found," "unclear insurance billing across telehealth").

The attack surface of a telehealth brand is its own operating model. JumpstartMD's strategy is to claim the territory those brands structurally cannot, not to out-market Hims & Hers.

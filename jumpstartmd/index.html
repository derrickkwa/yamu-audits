Why the gap is real, not a measurement artefact.

The 3.8× LTV delta we're seeing in JSMD's data is materially larger than that benchmark, consistent with GLP-1 category economics where a peer endorsement pre-qualifies for medication conversion. What Hims / Ro / LifeMD structurally cannot do

Telehealth commoditises a chronic-disease medication into an app transaction. Our view: that business model has a ceiling, and JumpstartMD's 14 physical clinics sit above it.

Moat 1

In-person monitoring. Weekly vitals, InBody body composition scan, labs on file. A telehealth platform can ship a prescription; it cannot deliver the clinical infrastructure that tracks whether the medication is working.

Moat 2

HRT + GLP-1 integrated care. No major telehealth player bundles hormonal and metabolic care under one clinical relationship. Building it requires clinicians, exam rooms, and labs: the physical infrastructure they have explicitly rejected.

Moat 3

17 years of practice tenure + 1.5M pounds lost. Credence-goods law: in categories where outcomes can't be verified pre-purchase, longevity substitutes for proof. No telehealth brand younger than 2020 can claim this.

Moat 4

Concierge PPO insurance service. Handles the paperwork for out-of-network reimbursement. Direct answer to VoC's #1 operational pain point in the category ("$99 cancellation fees at Found," "unclear insurance billing across telehealth").

Our read: the attack surface of a telehealth brand is its own operating model. We'd propose JumpstartMD's strategy be to claim the territory those brands structurally cannot, rather than try to out-market Hims & Hers.

Why we think creator-led acquisition closes part of the $120 → $459 LTV gap.

Referral members convert to medication at 34.7% because a peer-endorsement carries pre-qualification; the new member arrives already trusting the clinical model. Cold Meta ads carry none of that; paid members convert at 14.6%. Creator content sits between the two: it's mediated by someone the viewer has already chosen to follow, often someone who shares the viewer's own identity tension (PCOS, peri-menopausal, post-40 metabolic change). Our hypothesis is that creator-acquired members should land materially closer to referral economics than cold-ad economics, especially when the creator is a real patient rather than a generic content producer.

Why 4 creators Week 1, 6–8 by M3 — and not 15 or 20.

The numbers are anchored to the content-pillar structure, not picked arbitrarily. We've defined four messaging pillars for JSMD (food noise / perimenopause / clinician-not-bot / credence stack — see the next expandable). One creator per pillar at Week 1 = 4 creators. That's the minimum volume to get a clean read across pillars in a 30-day window. Scaling to 6–8 by M3 = 1.5–2 creators per pillar, which matches Found's per-angle creator ratio (11 ads ÷ 4+ angles ≈ 2–3 creators per angle) without matching Found's total volume.

The reason we stay under Found's total is the competitor analysis finding: "JumpstartMD will not out-volume Found on creator UGC, but can out-execute on the one thing creators can't deliver — clinical proof and integrated HRT. Out-execution on depth, not ideation on format." Found's whole operating model is creator volume. JSMD's edge is clinical depth (weekly vitals, InBody, HRT integration). Running 15–20 creators chases Found on Found's strengths — agency production scale, creator sourcing pipelines, creative ops — where we don't win. Running fewer than 4 means we can't test across pillars and the signal stays too noisy to steer the program.

What we'd propose building: start at 4 creators Week 1 (one per messaging pillar). Scale to 6–8 active partnerships by M3, rotating quarterly. Mix of Meta Creator Marketplace (Yamu has access), direct Instagram scouting, and member-turned-creator pipeline (real patients offered a partnership — the 17-year patient base is a sourcing moat Found structurally can't match). Medvi-style affiliate layer M3: tracked referral links + revenue share at/near Meta CAC (Shaan has stated willingness to pay at that level). Cadence: 2–3 new creator drops per month + 3–5 derivative cuts per drop for paid amplification. Pace to review against Found's durations: if our creator ads hit 60d at healthy CAC, we're on Found's trajectory with half the volume.
